The Story

I didn’t learn design in a classroom.

I learned it under pressure. Global teams. Impossible deadlines. Packaging that had to survive a factory floor and still sell on a supermarket shelf. Investor decks that had one shot in a boardroom. Reports that had to make a CFO stop, read, and care.

I spent 18 years doing the work that most designers talk about. FMCG. Pharma. Tech. Finance. NGOs. Retail. And along the way I learned something no design school teaches: the brands that win aren’t the prettiest ones. They’re the most consistent ones.

Consistency is not about templates. It’s about having a system — a visual language with rules, logic, and structure — that holds no matter who touches it or where it shows up next.

I’ve watched brands fall apart when they scaled. I’ve also watched what happens when a brand is built right from the start — how teams move faster, clients trust more, and everything just looks like it came from the same brilliant mind.

How I Think

Design is only as good as the thinking that shaped it.

Not just what I make — but how I make it matter.

Brand Strategy Thinking

Before I design anything, I ask what the brand needs to say. Positioning, tone, visual language — these aren’t separate conversations. They inform each other.

Systems Architecture

I build design systems with logic. Defined rules for colour, type, layout, imagery, and spacing — so the brand works as a whole, not a collection of one-off pieces.

Content Simplification

I take complex source material — dense reports, strategy documents, raw data — and restructure it into visual communication that earns attention and communicates clearly.

Production Intelligence

I know print specs, packaging dielines, digital formats, and platform requirements. My work doesn’t just look good in a design file. It survives the real world.

Stakeholder Fluency

I translate design decisions into business language. I present confidently, handle feedback constructively, and keep projects moving even when opinions diverge.

Cross-Platform Integrity

From an investor deck to a product label to a social post to a billboard — I design brands that look intentional everywhere.
